Domain 01

Security Readiness

01 Dedicated Workday security ownership is assigned for AI and automation-related changes.
02 Integration and report ISUs are documented, owned, and periodically reviewed.
03 Privileged access reviews occur on a defined cadence.
04 Security changes follow a documented approval and testing process.
05 Integration System Security Groups are standardized and reviewed for least privilege.
06 Sensitive data access for ISSGs is periodically reviewed.
07 Service accounts are inventoried with owner and purpose.
08 Terminated users are removed from report sharing, distribution, and schedule ownership.
09 Security audit findings are tracked to closure.
10 Teams can explain which security groups support critical AI-relevant workflows.
$> Agents inherit your security model. They do not improve it.
Domain 02

Business Process Readiness

01 Each major business process has a named owner who can approve AI or automation-related changes.
02 Business Process Security Policies are documented and compliant with internal controls.
03 Approval chains are documented, current, and explainable.
04 Exception handling is documented for critical processes.
05 Condition rules are documented with owner and purpose.
06 Business process changes follow a documented governance process.
07 Business Process Admins can explain the why and how behind routing, approvals, and security.
08 Reassign Tasks and delegation procedures are documented.
09 Unassigned business process tasks are monitored with an escalation path.
10 Teams know who owns resolution when a critical process fails.
$> An undocumented process cannot be safely automated.
Domain 03

Reporting & Insights Readiness

01 Critical and SOX reports have defined report tags and business owners.
02 Workday data is trusted enough for AI-assisted decisions.
03 Duplicate and copied reports are regularly reviewed, consolidated, or removed.
04 Calculated fields follow naming standards and are reviewed for DNU fields.
05 Report security is reviewed periodically, especially for sensitive data.
06 Key metrics have agreed-upon definitions.
07 Reporting requests follow a governance, intake, and prioritization process.
08 Report schedules are owned by ISUs or active users, not terminated workers.
09 Terminated users are removed from report sharing and report schedules.
10 Teams can explain how key metrics are calculated.
$> Agents will answer questions using the same reporting foundation your users rely on today.
Domain 04

Integration Readiness

01 All integrations are inventoried with owner, purpose, and criticality.
02 Integration technical and functional owners are identified and ISUs are assigned.
03 Monitoring is in place for critical integrations.
04 Failure notifications route to the correct technical and business owners.
05 Critical integrations have documented support and runbook procedures.
06 Vendors and contacts are clearly identified and current.
07 Upstream and downstream integration dependencies are understood.
08 Integration failures are reviewed, resolved, or documented with business impact.
09 PGP, SSH, and API keys are monitored for expiration and rotation.
10 Integration schedules and expiration dates are documented and owned by the appropriate ISUs.
$> MCP is not a replacement for integration governance.
Domain 05

Technical Foundation

01 The team understands where Workday Orchestrate could safely support process automation.
02 The team understands when Workday Extend is appropriate.
03 The team understands Workday APIs and integration guardrails.
04 AI governance discussions include HR, IT, security, and business owners.
05 Testing standards exist for configuration, integrations, and business process changes.
06 Change management is documented for technical and process changes.
07 Technical debt is actively tracked and prioritized.
08 Release testing is repeatable and risk-based.
09 Architecture decisions are documented.
10 Leadership supports modernization with budget and resource priority.
